## Structure & Dynamics  ·  23 labels  ·  244 instances

### `builds`  (54 songs)
The song progressively increases in energy, intensity, or complexity over a defined section. Short-term escalation, typically leading to a drop.

### `repetitive`  (33 songs)
A chord, phrase, or motif controls or anchors the entire song by repeating throughout. The repetition is structural, not incidental.

### `breakdown`  (23 songs)
A section where the song strips back or deconstructs, removing elements before rebuilding. Distinct from a drop — breakdown is the removal, drop is what follows.

### `chorus`  (19 songs)
The main identifiable repeated section of the song, typically the most singable and memorable part.

### `drops`  (18 songs)
A moment where tension releases into a new rhythmic or sonic phase. The song changes character at the drop point.

### `switches`  (16 songs)
The song contains distinct sections that change character or mood. The change is deliberate and emotionally significant, more extreme than simply having different parts.

### `story`  (14 songs)
The song has a narrative arc — it develops over time with a clear beginning, middle, and end or emotional journey.

### `layers`  (11 songs)
Multiple elements stacked in parallel simultaneously. Like parallel lines — each is independent but they run together.

### `steady`  (9 songs)
The rhythmic foundation of the song is consistent and reliable. The beat holds its structure without variation.

### `flashes`  (7 songs)
Brief intermittent moments that stand out — like lights turning on and off. Spotlight-style pops within the song.

### `outro`  (6 songs)
The closing section of the song. Notable for its quality, distinctiveness, or how it ends the song.

### `progression`  (6 songs)
The song develops continuously and organically into a more complete version of itself. Longer-term than builds — the song grows into what it becomes rather than escalating to a drop.

### `earned drop`  (4 songs)
A drop where tension was built so naturally and gradually that the listener did not consciously notice it building. The release feels inevitable and fully deserved.

### `takeover`  (4 songs)
A beat or element that starts in the background and gradually or suddenly becomes the dominant force of the song, taking over both the song and the listener.

### `on beat`  (4 songs)
See Structure & Dynamics.

### `intro`  (3 songs)
The opening section of the song is notable — either for how it sets up the song or for its own distinct quality.

### `merging`  (3 songs)
Two distinct elements or sections converge into one, like parallel lines meeting. The combination creates something new.

### `mixing`  (2 songs)
Different instruments or elements appear in isolation for short periods — never parallel at the same time — creating a shifting sonic landscape.

### `circular`  (2 songs)
The song loops back on itself — the beat or structure starts where it ends, creating a cyclical feeling.

### `climax`  (2 songs)
The song builds to a clearly identifiable peak moment — the highest point of energy or emotional intensity. Typically after a blossom, when the song is at its fullest.

### `tempo change`  (2 songs)
The song notably shifts tempo during its duration. The change is deliberate and audible.

### `blossom`  (1 songs)  ⬆ SUPER
**Components:** `builds` · `explosion`
[SUPER] A specific type of drop where tension from a single core element spreads outward simultaneously into many parts — like a flower opening or a bomb detonating. Components: builds + explosion.

### `pauses`  (1 songs)
Deliberate silence or rests are used as a structural feature. The quiet is intentional and musical.
